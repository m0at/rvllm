Findings
--------

1. **Decode is GEMM-bound, not attention-bound at batch=128.**
   CUTLASS SM120 blockwise FP8 GEMM accounts for ~98% of step time by
   extrapolation. Current memory 22222222aa010001 "attention/LM-head-
   bound" framing was derived from the pre-CUTLASS baseline; with
   CUTLASS default-on (commit fd8c674) the GEMM path is now fast
   enough to make attention the small tail, not the dominant cost.

2. **CUTLASS SM120 is memory-bound.** SM throughput 19% with L2
   throughput 20%. On a compute-bound kernel we'd see SM% → 80-95.
   This matches the earlier measurement: 101.8 TFLOPS at M=128 on
   Gemma 4 QKV shape is ~20% of GB10's FP8 TC peak. DRAM% reads 0
   because blockwise FP8 weights fit largely in L2 at this N/K; the
   L2 roundtrip at 20% is the throttle. This is why CUTLASS tile-
   autotune (task aa01001fcutlasat) is a real lever — a better
   MmaTileShape/ClusterShape that reuses weight columns across more
   output rows could push L2 utilization higher.

3. **FA2 FP8-KV decode kernel is smem-stalled.** SM% = 6.2%,
   L2% = 1.1%, L1% = 3.9%. Very low on every axis. The kernel tile
   is head_dim × BC × 4 bytes per K and per V = 64 KB for head_dim=256
   / BC=32, already at the 99 KB opt-in ceiling. Occupancy suffers
   because a single block hogs most of the per-SM smem budget
   (likely 1 block per SM). This is the right place to look for
   attention speedup when batch>=128 — options:
     a) Split-K over KV context to boost SM-count utilization
        (context_lens up to 4096 on Gemma 4, plenty to split).
     b) Smaller BC tile with register-resident K/V to free smem and
        allow 2+ blocks per SM.
     c) Native e2m1 MMA + 4-bit NVFP4 KV (task aa01001nvf4mma0)
        halves the smem tile — likely bumps occupancy.
   None of these are required at batch=128 because the kernel is
   only ~7% of step time; they become leverage when GEMM optimizes
   further or at higher batches.

4. **cuBLASLt (nvjet) still runs 6 times per step.** These are the
   non-blockscale GEMM ops: embedding gather, LM-head f16 × f16,
   and any GEMM falling outside the SM120 blockwise path (e.g.
   small-M or absent channelscale). nvjet at 506 μs × 6 ≈ 3 ms —
   not a bottleneck, noted for awareness.

5. **Scale-factor prep kernels (fill_sfa/sfb) are free.**
   ~6 μs each, <1% SM, called 240 times per step = ~2 ms total.
   SFA max-reduce-broadcast + SFB transpose (from loader's row-
   major b_chscale to CUTLASS MN-major SFB) add negligible overhead.

6. **No kernel serialization visible in the profile.** Graph-replay
   keeps the launch queue full; ncu observed per-kernel durations
   match sequential-launch expectation. The ~30 ms gap between
   extrapolated sum and wall-clock = overlap within a single CUDA
   stream (DRAM prefetch of weights for kernel N+1 during compute
   of kernel N), not a scheduling pathology.


Follow-up priorities (post this profile)
----------------------------------------

  HIGH:  CUTLASS SM120 tile-shape autotune (aa01001fcutlasat).
         Every % of CUTLASS improvement = ~0.98% of step time.

  MED:   FA2 FP8-KV decode smem-tuning — split-K or smaller BC.
         Pays off when CUTLASS gets faster or at batch≥256.

  LOW:   Investigate why some layer GEMMs fall to cuBLASLt nvjet
         when CUTLASS is loaded — if channelscale gating is too
         strict, forcing all 4 per-layer GEMMs through CUTLASS
         could close the remaining 2 ms nvjet tail.


Reproduction
------------

  cat > /tmp/ncu_bench.sh <<'SH'
  #!/bin/bash
  export RVLLM_MODEL_DIR=/path/to/gemma-4-31b-it-fp8-block
  export RVLLM_KERNELS_DIR=/path/to/rvllm/kernels
  export RVLLM_CUTLASS_SM120_SO=$RVLLM_KERNELS_DIR/sm_121/libcutlass_sm120.so
  export RVLLM_ARENA_GB=40 RVLLM_F16_KV=0
  export RVLLM_BATCH=128 RVLLM_ITERS=3 RVLLM_WARMUP=1
  exec /path/to/rvllm/v3/target/release/rvllm-bench
  SH
  chmod +x /tmp/ncu_bench.sh
  sudo /usr/local/cuda/bin/ncu \
    --launch-count 1500 \
    --section SpeedOfLight --section LaunchStats \
    --section Occupancy --section MemoryWorkloadAnalysis \
    -o /tmp/ncu_decode /tmp/ncu_bench.sh

  # Analyze:
  sudo /usr/local/cuda/bin/ncu --import /tmp/ncu_decode.ncu-rep \
    --csv --page details > /tmp/ncu_details.csv

Note: ncu profiling on GB10 requires root (RmProfilingAdminOnly=1 in
/proc/driver/nvidia/params). Per-kernel profiling slowdown is heavy
(the 1500-launch run didn't finish in 20 min); snapshot after ~60
kernels is already representative of the per-kernel cost structure.
