updated rvLLM and cleaned up the public repo/docs.

current public comparison is a simple apples-to-apples H100 run on Qwen2.5-7B f16 against:
- stock vLLM
- rvllm-lite
- rvLLM

latest verified numbers

direct engine, 256 output tokens
- N=1: rvLLM 120.6 tok/s, stock vLLM 133.7
- N=16: rvLLM 1,648.9 tok/s, stock vLLM 1,934.5
- N=32: rvLLM 3,170.0 tok/s, stock vLLM 3,197.1

HTTP, 200 requests, concurrency 32, max_tokens=256
- stock vLLM: 2,861.9 tok/s
- rvLLM: 2,723.2 tok/s
- rvllm-lite: 131.8 tok/s

the useful result for me was not just rvLLM vs vLLM. it was that rvllm-lite stays near stock vLLM on direct engine, then falls apart over HTTP. that makes the serving/scheduler overhead much easier to see.

other changes since the last writeup
- reserve-based safe-max VRAM sizing in rvLLM startup
- fixed the FA3 v3 decode crash
- updated README, github pages, and paper to the current benchmark set
- rebuilt the paper PDFs and pushed everything to main

still true: rvLLM is close at N=32 direct engine and close over HTTP, but it is not beating stock vLLM consistently yet. the remaining work is mostly direct-engine tuning: GEMM, attention, and scheduler behavior.
