Explanation: Angular momentum is a vector quantity that describes the rotational equivalent of linear momentum, representing the total momentum of a time-derivative with respect to an axis. It can be defined as the sum of each particle's moment of inertia times its angular velocity about the same axis; mathematically expressed as L = Σmr² × ω for point particles (or continuous bodies) where r is the perpendicular distance from the axis and p is the instantaneous linear momentum.
